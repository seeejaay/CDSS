Disease Pathophysiology

Overview

Type 2 diabetes mellitus: Insulin resistance, impaired insulin secretion, and abnormalities in glucose metabolism.

Risk Factors

Age, obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, ethnicity, and gestational diabetes.

Complications

Microvascular complications (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy) and macrovascular complications (cardiovascular disease, stroke, peripheral vascular disease).

Diabetes Management

Individualized based on patient characteristics, age, comorbidities, and diabetes duration.

Recommended HbA1c levels for glycemic control and risk reduction of complications.

Medication Management

Oral antidiabetic agents

Mechanism of action, indications, dosing, side effects, and contraindications for medications such as metformin, sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors, SGLT-2 inhibitors, and GLP-1 receptor agonists.

Insulin therapy

Types of insulin, dosing regimens, insulin initiation, titration algorithms, injection techniques, and hypoglycemia management.

Combination Therapy

Strategies for combining oral medications and/or insulin to achieve glycemic targets while minimizing sied effects and hypoglycemia risk.
