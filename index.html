Disease Pathophysiology

Overview

Type 2 diabetes mellitus: Insulin resistance, impaired insulin secretion, and abnormalities in glucose metabolism.

Risk Factors

Age, obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, ethnicity, and gestational diabetes.

Complications

Microvascular complications (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy) and macrovascular complications (cardiovascular disease, stroke, peripheral vascular disease).

Diabetes Management

Individualized based on patient characteristics, age, comorbidities, and diabetes duration.

Recommended HbA1c levels for glycemic control and risk reduction of complications.

Medication Management

Oral antidiabetic agents

Mechanism of action, indications, dosing, side effects, and contraindications for medications such as metformin, sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors, SGLT-2 inhibitors, and GLP-1 receptor agonists.

Insulin therapy

Types of insulin, dosing regimens, insulin initiation, titration algorithms, injection techniques, and hypoglycemia management.

Combination Therapy

Strategies for combining oral medications and/or insulin to achieve glycemic targets while minimizing sied effects and hypoglycemia risk.

Lifestyle Modifications

Dietary Recommendations

Macronutrient composition, carbohydrate counting, glycemic index, portion control, and dietary patterns (e.g., Mediterranean diet, DASH diet).

Physical Activity

Types of exercise, duration, intensity, frequency, and incorporation into daily routine.

Weight Management

Strategies for weight loss, maintenance, and prevention of obesity-related complications.

Monitoring & Follow-Up

  • Self-monitoring of blood glucose (SMBG): Frequency, timing, target ranges, interpretation of results, and adjustments to treatment.
  • Laboratory monitoring: Recommended intervals for monitoring HbA1c, lipid profile, renal function, liver function, and other relevant parameters.
  • Regular follow-up visits: Schedule for routine check-ups, medication adjustments, education, and support.

Complications Management

  • Prevention and management of diabetes-related complications: Screening, early detection, treatment, and referral to specialists.
  • Cardiovascular risk reduction: Blood pressure targets, lipid management, antiplatelet therapy, and lifestyle interventions.
